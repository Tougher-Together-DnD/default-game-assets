# Diving Suit

## Depth
The deeper a surface-dwelling creature travels into [The Sea](https://app.roll20.net/compendium/dnd5e/Unusual%20Environments#h-The%20Sea), the more [Hostile](https://app.roll20.net/compendium/dnd5e/Mysterious%20Islands#h-Hostile) the [Environment](https://app.roll20.net/compendium/dnd5e/Kobolds#h-Environment) becomes. Those venturing into the depths must keep the following natural forces in mind.

### Illumination
When it comes to light, [The Sea](https://app.roll20.net/compendium/dnd5e/Unusual%20Environments#h-The%20Sea) is divided into three layers:

**Sunlight Zone.** The sunlight zone extends from the water’s surface to a depth of 650 feet. This area of [The Sea](https://app.roll20.net/compendium/dnd5e/Unusual%20Environments#h-The%20Sea) has the same natural [Illumination](https://app.roll20.net/compendium/dnd5e/Illumination#h-Illumination) [Conditions](https://app.roll20.net/compendium/dnd5e/Player%27s%20Handbook#h-Conditions) as above [The Water](https://app.roll20.net/compendium/dnd5e/Well%20of%20Urd#h-The%20Water).

**Twilight Zone.** The twilight zone is between the depths of 650 and 1,000 feet. When the sunlight zone above is bathed in natural bright light, the twilight zone is filled with dim light. If the sunlight zone is filled with dim light or [Darkness](https://app.roll20.net/compendium/dnd5e/Darkness#h-Darkness), the twilight zone is dark.

**Midnight Zone.** Any depth below 1,000 feet is considered the midnight zone, since no natural light from the surface penetrates this deep.

### Pressure and Temperature
[Water](https://app.roll20.net/compendium/dnd5e/The%20Environment#h-Water) pressure, or the weight of water on top of a creature or object, increases with depth. Conversely, temperature decreases as depth increases. The pressure and temperature’s [Effects](https://app.roll20.net/compendium/dnd5e/Designing%20Simple%20Traps#h-Effects) on creatures without a [Swimming](https://app.roll20.net/compendium/dnd5e/Unusual%20Environments#h-Swimming) speed at depths below 100 feet can be found in the “Unusual Environments” section in chapter 5 of the Dungeon Master’s Guide.

### Optional Rule: Pressure and [Objects](https://app.roll20.net/compendium/dnd5e/Objects#h-Objects) 
With this optional rule, [Characters](https://app.roll20.net/compendium/dnd5e/The%20Wild%20Beyond%20the%20Witchlight#h-Characters) who dive deep in the ocean require specialized [Equipment](https://app.roll20.net/compendium/dnd5e/Chapter%205%20-%20Backgrounds#h-Equipment) that can withstand the ocean’s pressure. Nonmagical [Objects](https://app.roll20.net/compendium/dnd5e/Objects#h-Objects) not made to withstand [The Water](https://app.roll20.net/compendium/dnd5e/Well%20of%20Urd#h-The%20Water) pressure are destroyed at various depths, as determined by the material used to create them. This destructive depth is presented for various materials on the [Objects](https://app.roll20.net/compendium/dnd5e/Objects#h-Objects) and [Water](https://app.roll20.net/compendium/dnd5e/The%20Environment#h-Water) Pressure table. [Objects](https://app.roll20.net/compendium/dnd5e/Objects#h-Objects) made of other materials break at the DM’s discretion.

## Features
This Suit is crafted from thick plaster coated cotton, copper, and brass. It covers the body and is quite heavy. When used with a surface air pump the Diving Suit can take a person to a depth of 500 feet without the person suffering damage or drowning.

### Don/Doff like Armor
It takes 10 minutes to Don, and 5 minutes to Doff a Diving Suit. Adds +2 to AC.

### Difficult Terrain
To walk 5 feet on land, requires a DC 10 Strength Check.

When underwater movement speed is half, unless a player has a swim speed. In accordance with Underwater Combat.
